linkNYC


what's good? what's bad?





object qualities

+ Big fans of charging port
+ Some people liked the transit displays
+ Almost every site featured a lot of leaning, propping, and other usages of the kiosk for support
+ Lots of people liked to use the tool for video watching
+ Lots of people make phone calls, both local and international (one person said he only called his doctor from a LinkNYC phone, never his cell phone)

- Inability to print anything!
- Challenging to type on
- Charging port time VERY contentious in certain places, and bizarrely usually between people who know each other
- Challenging to understand privacy policy. Many people were surprised to hear about the data collection and unsure what that even meant
- Relative ignorance of being connected to the WiFi, especially for car drivers
- Serious concern over video surveillance, particularly with Spanish speakers
- Negative reputation and distrust of using WiFi in certain areas
- Clustering of things around kiosk results in an accessibility issue
- Neighbor animosity towards loud music playing and loitering
- Relatively hit or miss servicing of the tablets. In 50 visits, I identified 18 malfunctioning kiosk tablets
- Serious opposition from street vendors and business owners, especially in Times Square, citing kiosks attracting types of people that drive business away

takeaways: public wifi on the street

+ Ease of accessibility
+ Globalized system of telecommunication
+ Public charging function

- Relative overdemand of resources in certain areas (especially for charging)
- Lack of productive capabilities like printing and typing
- Mismatch of streetside locations to land use/economic activities
- Concern over ability of system to surveil users
- Challenging to understand privacy policy, when and where users are on the network, and other mechanics of the system



takeaways: public wifi, more broadly

Public WiFi Locations should interpret land use and economic patterns in their vicinity to locate more strategically Public WiFi solutions should include the robust telecommunications capacities of LinkNYC, especially the ability to communicate with other countries Public WiFi solutions need shade, heated, and cooled spaces Public WiFi solutions should include the ability to charge devices on the go, like in the current kiosks Public WiFi solutions should be spatially distributed according to the needs of the city and not related to the former locations of payphones Public WiFi solutions need to better communication their ability to surveil users and collect data, and otherwise better explain their business models
